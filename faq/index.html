What kind of power outlets does Taniti use?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

Are there restrictions on alcohol sales?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

What is the legal drinking age on Taniti?

The drinking age is 18, though it is not strictly enforced.

Is English widely spoken?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, in rural areas, especially among older residents, very little English is spoken.

What kind of medical facilities are available?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist international visitors.

Is Taniti safe for tourists?

Violent crime is very rare. However, petty crimes like pickpocketing have increased slightly with the rise in tourism.

Should I be aware of national holidays?

Taniti observes many national holidays. Tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency is used on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks offer currency exchange, and major credit cards are widely accepted.

Is there a difference between euros, yen and U.S. Dollars?

Yes, check with your local bank if you have any questions.

Where is Taniti located?

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific Ocean, known for its lush rainforests, white sandy beaches, and an active volcano.

What is the size and population of Taniti?

Taniti has an area of less than 500 square miles and an indigenous population of around 20,000 people.

How do I get to Taniti?

Most visitors arrive by air, though a small cruise ship also docks at Yellow Leaf Bay once per week.

Is there transportation on the island?

Yes. Taniti has public buses in the city, rental cars near the airport, taxis, hotel shuttles, and bikes for rent. The capital, Taniti City, is very walkable.

What are the top things to do in Taniti?

Popular activities include beach lounging, rainforest hikes, volcano tours, snorkeling, zip-lining, fishing charters, and visiting local museums and art galleries.
